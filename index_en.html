About

Biography

Kei Sano studied medicine at The Jikei University School of Medicine, completed clinical training at The Jikei University Hospital, and is currently enrolled in the PhD program at The Jikei University Graduate School. As a Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) Research Fellow (DC2 and PD), he continues his research, particularly publishing numerous international papers in the fields of ophthalmology and AI, and public health.

Selected Works

Latent Retinal Archetype

A novel framework for identifying characteristic patterns (archetypes) in high-dimensional retinal imaging data. By embedding retinal measurements into a latent space and decomposing them into archetypal components, this method enables better understanding of retinal structural patterns with aging.
